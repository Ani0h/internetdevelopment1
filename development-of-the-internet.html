YEAR
1950
Sputnik First artificial earth satellite to be launched into outer space. This was done by the soviet union on October 4 1957. This started the space race.
First Modem The first Modem is invented by Bell, an American company. The Bell 101 Modem was first released in 1958 for Military use. It was used for SAGE by NORAD In 1959 it was released for commercial use.
Creation of ARPA February 7 1958, USA creates ARPA in response to Sputnik. The Advanced Research Projects Agency was created to prevent technological surprise.
1960
Packet Switching Concept Packet switching is transmitting packets or blocks of  broken down data  over various digital networks, Where the receiving device reassembles them. This enables for faster data transfer. Packet switching was invented by Paul Baran and Donald Davies in the early 1960s.
ASCII ASCII stands for  American Standard Code for Information Interchange. It assigns numeric values to letters numbers punctuation, generally any character in the computer system. On June 17, 1963, ASCII was accepted as the American standard code.
First WAN (wide area network)via dial up The first known WAN was created by the U.S. Air Force (NORAD) in the late 1950s to interconnect sites in the Semi-Automatic Ground Environment (SAGE) radar defence system.
Start of ARPAnet 1966 was the start of ARPAnet. It was invented to facilitate resource sharing between remote computers. The idea was to link computers over a telephone wire. Arpanet consisted of  the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), the Stanford Research Institute, the University of California, Santa Barbara (UCSB), and the University of Utah.
First Data packet sent In late 1969, a team of UCLA graduate students under the leadership of professor Leonard Kleinrock sent the first packet-switched message between two computers
1970
Email Electronic mail is invented by Ray Tomlinson in 1971. He was working on Arpanet at the time.
TCP/IP transmission-control protocol/Internet protocol (TCP/IP). TCP/IP,  first tested on ARPANET in 1977. created by Vinton Cerf and Bob Kahn.
non us ARPAnet NORSAR was officially opened in 1970
Internet The term internet is coined in 1974.
The term “Internet” was first used in 1974 to describe a single global TCP/IP network
1980
DNS Domain Name System. Invented in 1983 by Paul Mockapetris. DNS translates domain names to IP addresses 
WWW (1989) is created Tim Burners Lee, the father of the internet creates the World Wide Web and makes it available to the public free of charge.
HTTP  Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP). It uses encryption for secure. Widely used on the internet communication over a computer network
Mosaic – first browser Mosaic was the first browser. 
MP3 developed MP3's are developed, shrinking the size of large files
VOIP Voice over internet protocol, allows calls to be made via the internet
First Mobile Phone with internet connection The first mobile phone with internet changed the face of computing. Moving technology was now available which made the internet more accessible

Wi-Fi

Blockchain A blockchain is a computer program that serves as a shared, unchangeable ledger that is completely transparent, highly secure, and viewable only by those on a permitted network.
Online Collaboration Tools – Teams; Zoom Allows for people to work remotely, teams and Zoom are made available due to Covid, this is successful when used hand in hand with cloud computing
